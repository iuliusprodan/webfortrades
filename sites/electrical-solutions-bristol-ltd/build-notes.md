## Hero decision - typographic / solid (no photo behind the headline)

The 9 Google Places photos were each viewed with vision:
- 01 consumer unit board - clean, well-lit, FINISHED, but a busy technical close-up.
- 02 socket WIP on carpet with tools / exposed wiring - work-in-progress, excluded.
- 04 multifunction tester held against a socket strip - shows testing, hand visible.
- 05 earth bonding clamp on a copper pipe outdoors - gritty technical detail.
- 06 earthing clamp in a ground box with soil - earthing work.
- 07 two sockets on a bare/peeling-paint wall - work-in-progress, excluded.
- 08 outdoor switched fused spur on a stone wall, neat conduit - finished, tidy.
- 09 relay/smart modules + tool on a workbench, loose parts - detail crop, excluded.
- 10 Lawrence smiling at the back of his fully-kitted SMARTVAN holding a Kewtech tester - a PORTRAIT
  of a person with van clutter behind.

None is a clean "completed lit room" showcase (electricians' work is mostly behind walls / in boards).
The skill permits a strong typographic/solid hero when photos are weak/few, and a premium editorial-serif
violet field best carries the high-end-domestic-lighting positioning. So the hero is typographic on a
deep-amethyst-to-near-black gradient with a faint engraved filament-line motif (the lighting signature).
The strong finished-work shots (01, 08, 04, 05, 06) plus the personal-brand portrait (10) go in the
gallery. This also avoids the WIP-on-bare-walls and person-in-hero problems entirely.

  `#F4F2F7`. Clearly distinct from AC's warm aubergine + brass (cooler/brighter violet + silver). Only
  jewel-tone in the batch. Not green, not eggplant-brown.

- Signature motif: the "filament" divider (violet-to-pewter gradient hairline + centred glowing dot),
  the lighting-design analogue of Damo's deck-board rule.
